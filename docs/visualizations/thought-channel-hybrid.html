Thought-Channel Hybrid

A block diagram for a search-native hybrid: shared transformer attention for exact token interaction, plus multiple fully active thought channels that keep separate latent hypotheses alive inside recurrent or state-space workspaces.

Core Idea

Keep attention where it is best: exact binding, retrieval, and final answer formation. Put iterative search into separate always-on latent channels that can refine competing hypotheses without serializing every branch into tokens.

Why Hybrid

  • Attention handles exact token interaction and context retrieval.
  • SSM or recurrent workspaces hold per-channel working memory cheaply.
  • Shared trunk compute is reused across all hypotheses.
  • Cross-channel attention is paid periodically, not everywhere.

Not Standard MoE

  • All thought channels stay active rather than sparsely gating a few experts.
  • The key primitive is separate hypothesis state, not only specialization.
  • Score, prune, merge, and halt become first-class runtime operations.

Why It Could Beat An External Harness

  • Hypotheses stay latent instead of being re-serialized into text.
  • The shared attention trunk can serve all channels at once.
  • Per-channel refinement can be much cheaper than full model re-entry.
  • Compare and merge happen over richer internal state than text traces allow.

Main Risks

  • Channels may interfere or collapse into near-duplicates.
  • Scoring and pruning may be unstable without strong training signals.
  • The controller may waste compute on latent churn instead of useful search.
  • Interpretability becomes harder than in an external explicit GoT harness.
