# Parcae 16k Scale Rung

Date: 2026-04-20

This run promoted the fixed-policy Parcae proof-ladder lanes to a longer
`16,384`-step rung on the same FineWeb-EDU MaxText surface:

- `attention`
- `parcae-bx`
- `parcae-rgrp-control`

The goal was to test whether the shorter-run positive signal from
`parcae-rgrp-control` survived a larger training budget and whether the Parcae
outer loop scaffold alone remained competitive.

## Interpretation

- `parcae-rgrp-control` won every eval checkpoint against both controls.
- The final RGRP-control advantage was `0.089` loss over attention and `0.025`
  loss over `parcae-bx`.
- `parcae-bx` also beat attention by `0.064` loss, so the Parcae loop scaffold
  remains a live architectural control independent of the rotary gated
  recurrent state update primitive.
- RGRP-control still pays a speed tax on the fast-step metric: about `71.7%` of
  attention median steady throughput and about `86.2%` of `parcae-bx`.
- The gap between median steady throughput and log duration shows that this TPU
  rung is still dominated by more than the hot matmul/scan path. Do not use this
  run for CUDA or wall-clock efficiency claims.

## Decision

Promote `parcae-rgrp-control` to the next proof-ladder rung as the quality
leader, while keeping `parcae-bx` as the required scaffold control.

The next run should either:

- replicate this `16k` rung across at least two more seeds before scaling, or
- move to a larger model/data rung only if the cost budget can tolerate carrying
  all three lanes.

Do not drop `parcae-bx`: it is a strong control and explains how much of the
quality gain comes from the Parcae looped scaffold rather than RGRP itself.
